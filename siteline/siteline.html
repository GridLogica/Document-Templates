Abstract

This inquiry investigates the intersection of sensory fidelity, cognitive architecture, and pedagogical intent within immersive learning environments. As spatial computing moves from a novelty to a formal educational medium, the requirement for standardized assessment frameworks becomes critical.

Through the lens of design psychology and Level 5 DET standards, this research establishes "Efficient Fidelity" thresholds—defining the exact point where visual and auditory immersion supports, rather than distracts from, conceptual retention.

The resulting specification provides a formal roadmap for designers and educators to transition from traditional flat-screen paradigms to embodied, spatial learning systems that are ethical, effective, and measurable.

01. Core Framework

Typographic and Structural Integrity

The GridLogica system requires a rigorous approach to information density. In spatial environments, the user's cognitive load is directly impacted by the clarity of the visual hierarchy. This applies equally to our technical documentation.

"Immersion is not a feature of the hardware, but a state of cognitive alignment where the interface dissolves into the conceptual intent."

Embodied Cognition

When a user moves within a spatial field, their understanding of the content is mediated by their physical presence. This is the cornerstone of Embodied Pedagogy. We must design transitions and interactions that respect the user's proprioceptive sense.

Traditional metrics often fail in these environments because they ignore the temporal and spatial nature of the learning act. We propose a move toward gesture-based proof of mastery.
